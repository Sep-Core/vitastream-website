Members

We are a cross-disciplinary team combining product thinking, industrial design, embedded systems, and software engineering. Our work focuses on practical sustainability: making graywater reuse reliable, safe, and easy to adopt in everyday settings. We iterate fast with prototypes, validate decisions through testing, and keep user experience simple and transparent. By turning “waste” into measurable savings, we aim to help households and small public facilities reduce both water usage and long-term costs.

Team’s Story

  • Insight → We noticed that sink graywater is often clean enough for non-drinking reuse, yet it is routinely discarded.
  • Prototype → We built early diversion and filtration modules to validate pH/turbidity thresholds and treatment steps.
  • Iterate → Through testing, we improved stability, reduced false diversion, and redesigned the collection path for cleaner supernatant.
  • Vision → We aim to make water savings measurable—turning daily routines into transparent, real cost reductions.
  • Next → Expand detectable parameters, optimize control algorithms, and strengthen structural/electrical integration for deployment.

why "Vitastream" (click the triangle to learn more)

Vitastream combines “Vita” (life/vitality) and “Stream” (water flow), highlighting that our system gives wastewater a second life by turning graywater into a clean, reusable stream for non-drinking uses like toilet flushing and irrigation.
